Composite Lamina Property & Failure Calculator

This tool allows you to calculate key properties of a composite lamina and perform a Tsai-Hill failure analysis based on material properties and applied stresses. Enter the properties of the matrix and fiber materials, the fiber volume fraction, and the applied stresses to get the calculated composite properties and failure status.

Limitations and Assumptions

This calculator uses simplified models (Rule of Mixtures, Halpin-Tsai) and the Tsai-Hill failure criterion. Key assumptions include:

  • The composite is a unidirectional lamina.
  • Perfect bonding exists between the fibers and the matrix.
  • Materials are homogeneous and isotropic within their respective phases (matrix and fiber).
  • Calculations are based on linear elastic material behavior.
  • The Tsai-Hill criterion is an interaction criterion and does not predict the mode of failure.
Units and Conventions

Please use the following units for inputs:

  • Young's Modulus (E): GPa
  • Shear Modulus (G): GPa
  • Density (ρ): g/cm³
  • Volume Fraction (Vf): Dimensionless (between 0 and 1)
  • Poisson's Ratio (ν): Dimensionless
  • Applied Stresses (σ₁, σ₂, τ₁₂): MPa
  • Material Strengths (Xt, Xc, Yt, Yc, S12): MPa

The convention for material directions is:

  • Direction 1: Longitudinal (along the fibers)
  • Direction 2: Transverse (perpendicular to the fibers)

Note on units: The calculator performs calculations assuming consistent units. Ensure all inputs are in the specified units (GPa, g/cm³, MPa) for accurate results. Conversions between GPa and MPa (1 GPa = 1000 MPa) may be necessary depending on your source data.
